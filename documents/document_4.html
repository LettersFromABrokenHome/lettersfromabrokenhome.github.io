Believe in Yourself

Written on 03/04/2025

Uploaded on 2/27/2026



This speech was written for one of my friends once she told me she lacked confidence in herself to do what she needed to do. I sent her this in an attempt to help show her you can do whatever you set your mind to. The first step was always the “I can do this”.







Throughout your life, people can, have and will, tell you you're not good enough. You're a failure. You won't make it. You're an idiot. And they're right... only if you let them be.


See Henry Ford said it best, "Whether you think you can, or you think you can't, you're right". We all have our opinions, and none of them are wrong. That's what an opinion is. It's our own belief that we have formulated. It's what we think. If you want another good opinion, listen to how I opened again. All of those are opinions. People will try to influence your opinion. Sometimes for the worse, like my opening examples, and sometimes for the better.


I'll get back to that in a minute, but I'd like to tell you a story first. It's called the little engine that could, now it's been a while since I've last read it, and as I wrote this I couldn't find any copies of it. But I still have the same general idea, so bear with me if i get a thing or two wrong.


There was a giant set of carriages that had toys and candies for the boys and girls. It needed to go over this hill. Strong train engines came by, looked at it, and said it was too difficult, they couldn't do it, it was impossible. But this little train, smaller than all the rest said "I can do it". It repeated the mantra "I think I can" to itself. And it did. It pulled the cars over the hill.


The little blue engine was only able to pull the cars across the hill because it believed in itself. It set it's mind to a goal, and succeeded. It didn't let the others beliefs affect it.


I promise all of this is relevant to the topic I'm trying to discuss.


I have one last thing before this all ties together. There's an old saying, "if you say something enough times, you'll start to believe it". If you told yourself so many times that your vreat great great grandfather was Abraham Lincon enough, you'll believe it. You won't be able to separate fact from fiction. It's become your truth. Your opinion.


Finally time to wrap these random trinkets with a bow.


 People will try to knock you down, but you need to get back on your feet. You can't let their mantra of ill words become your truth. Their opinions can't become facts. If you want to prove your haters wrong, you can't do it by listening to them. Be the little engine that could. Chant words of encouragement. And cross your hill.


This I can't expect to help everyone because it's a very difficult mindset to get into. But it's a lovely one once you've moved in. Don't listen to what others say. Don't let the popular girls tell you your clothes are ugly or your hair is ratty. Don't care. Ignore them. They only hate on you because they hate themselves. Only care about what your close friends say. And nothing else.


One last relevant thing, its one of my mottos, my words to live by. You can't hate anyone else, unless you can hate yourself. You can't think someone else is dumb, unless you can think you're dumb first. You can't make jokes about others, unless you can make jokes about you. You can't love, unless you can love yourself.
