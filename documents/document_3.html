Pit of Despair

Written on 05/03/2025

Uploaded on 02/25/2026



This speech was a reflection for myself when I felt like my closest friend had abandoned me. I realized then, who my true friends were.








Do me a favor. And close your eyes. Picture a dry empty desert. A flat expanse for as far as the eye can see. Now picture alongside you, are your friends. I promise you there are folks around you even if it doesn't seem like it. Now this environment you're in is a literal hell. Its hot, humid, a torture and a burden to walk along. But it seems to you like heaven, cuz around you are your friends. You don't say a word. The only sound is the ground beneath your feet. Not a word is spoken and yet you're happy. It seems like nothing can go wrong. But then something does. You trip. And fall. Your knees are scraped and bloody. Your hands are likewise. It's nothing you can't pick yourself up from.


It should hurt, you think. But it doesn't the pain you feel isn't comparable to how much joy your friends company brings you. You begin to pick yourself up. You look around, see your friends with outstretched arms. Extending to help get you up off the ground and on your feet. But then you see it. One of your most closest, and trusted friends is walking away. They either didn't notice you fell, or didn't care. You then feel the hell you're in. The company of your friends isn't enough to douse the pain you feel. You pick yourself up to chase after them. To get their attention, to ask them to stay with you. But you fall again. Harder this time. More than your knees hands and elbows are scraped. You're wounded, all over. Pain seeping from every opening in your body. You feel the hot son beating down on you.


The person you chased doesn't care. They're too busy. Living life too fast. While it's true, the faster you go through life you don't fall as often, but it also means when you do you fall harder. These folks never stop and smell the roses. They never take life for what it is. They rush to the next stop without looking around. They can't lament their journey cuz they never knew what they missed. They don't deserve to walk alongside you. They refuse to acknowledge your pain.


Your other friends catch up to you. They offer their help to get you moving again. You try to stand up on your own. But it feels like you swallowed a lead brick. You can't. Not alone. They grab your arms and hoist you up. They drape your arms behind their necks, so they can help you along. One of them offers you their canteen, you respectfully decline. After some time you're able to walk again. Your friends want to keep helping you, knowing you're not able to continue on your own, they know you haven't finished healing. But you ignore their warnings. And walk. It doesn't take long before you fall.


This time you're not on your hands and knees. You're face down. You're eventually able to raise your head, and realize you fell down a sinkhole. You see your friends looking down. Wondering how they can help. They're not all accounted for. Some are missing. They must have went off without you, you think. You look. You're at lease 12 feet in the pit. You want to get out. You try and think of how. Then it hits you. In your bag you carried a pair of climbing picks. Why not use those to escape this chasm you found yourself stuck in. You begin to climb, its tiring, sure, but its quick. You're at the top in no time. But just before you reach the top, you slip.


You fall.  Your friends try to grab you. They fail. They can't help you know, you and they know it. Some more of your so called friends desert you. When you finally pick yourself back up you realize the hole has grown. Its deeper now. Nearing 20 feet. You look through your backpack once again. You find a grappling hook. You know this won't get you to the top, but you ignorantly try anyway. You aim, you fire. You land the shot. And the hook pulls you up. But you're ascending too quickly. You pass out from the sudden elevation change. Your unconscious body loses grip of the device. And you once again fall.


Much like before, this pit is much deeper than when you last came to. You look around, its so much deeper than before. You don't even bother trying to estimate. But in the distance, way up high. The remaining stragglers stay, hoping you resurge. The fact so many left you for dead burdens you. Another brick of lead in your stomach. You know you must carry on. So you look through your bag of tricks, and find one last item. A pickaxe. You see this and immediately know what to do. You plan to dig yourself a staircase out. You begin your labor. The sun beats down. Hotter than Hades. You very slowly carve your way back to the top. When you get there maybe only two or three stand alongside you.


They congratulate you on your victory. Your success. Your overcoming of pain and suffering, because when you return alongside your friends. The sun's heat dies away. You no longer notice your thirst. Your anguish fades like a dying star. You're happy once more.


You keep walking and find those who left you. They've fallen into pits like you did, and knowing the toil it caused you, their leaving, you stay alongside your fellow man. You await for their return, and like they are prodigal son, you celebrate. Not long does it take for you all to be reunited, and all to no longer burden. You will still fall, but you're more resilient now. You can face harder struggles. You can brave the unknown. You can save yourself, and others.


As the sun fades, and you and your friends walk together, hand and hand, into the silent night. You look back. You see the pits you and your buddies left behind. A sign of your struggles. And every pit a scar showing you where you've been, and where you're going. You look back and think "This journey was good." As that thought entangles your brain, you realize; amongst your friends, not a word was spoken.
