Ethics has always been a funny concept to me. You need to do what is morally correct, but ensure that no one gets hurt in the process. They try and teach you this in kindergarten, and early grade school. But no class can prepare you for ethics put to use. I stand by that.


See when you're faced with a moral dilemma where doing the right thing ethically hurts a lot of people in the process. What do you do? Taken an oath to not share a friend's secrets, I faced this dilemma in high-school. But my friend's secrets quickly turned dark. I feared for their safety. Do I stand by my promise of "word is gold, I'll never utter", or spill the beans and tell a teacher.


The question I just posed tore at me all day. An internal war. Do I report this, and risk ruining what we have, or keep silent and risk my friend doing something bad. It ate at me. Tore me piece from piece until my insides felt like they came out of an industrial shredder. Ultimately I decided; "I'd be better for my friend to be mad at me and never wanna talk to me than for them to do something they won't be able to regret."


Talking to the teacher my insides turned into pudding. A jumbled mess inside of me. A nervous feeling in my gut. I went and told the teacher. He informed me we needed to go to guidance. And we did. As I rode home that afternoon I knew in my heart and accepted that my friend and I would never be the same. Yet I somehow reconciled with myself that this was the better option. I felt no remorse for my actions.


In fact I felt easier. Happier almost. Terrified of what I would lose, but I had solace in the fact that I could have stopped my friend form doing something unthinkable. The internal conflict died and it felt a weight had been lifted off my shoulders. I had carried a cross all day and asked God to remove the burden from me. It felt as if He did.


But here comes the problem that rests inside with the concept of ethics. The thing that is right may hurt people along the way. To quote one of my all time favorite bands, "It's something unpredictable, but in the end is right". They never prepare you for this. The moral dilemma inside, the horrible feeling when I spoke to the teacher that what I was doing was wrong because I violated my friend's trust. But in the end I realized what I did was the right choice. No matter how terrible it felt.


Ladies and gentlemen, when you feel a burden on your shoulders pray to God. He will provide. He's carried you safe thus far and will carry you safe throughout your days. He'll whisper and tell you what you need to do if you ask and pray.
