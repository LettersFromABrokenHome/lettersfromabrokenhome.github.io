Believe

Written on 11/27/2025

Uploaded on 01/22/2026



This speech was written for one of my friends after she said to me; “How strong are you in your faith? Mine is weak, because I struggle to believe in something that gives me no evidence it's there”. In response, I tried to prove, as best I could, that God is real and there for us. 







Excuse me, sir or ma'am, do you have a moment to talk about our Lord and Savior, Jesus Christ? I frequently see this question memed on the internet, captioned along an image of some animal trying to hop a fence or open a door. As parodied as this question is, I pose it genuinely; do you have a moment to talk about Jesus? If you're not Catholic, or Protestant or any other sect of Christianity, I'm sorry this speech is not for you. If you are, and you've become distant from God, or want to join the faith, then do you have the time? The answer should always be yes. You should always have the time for God.


These words I am writing right now, are not for those who are strong in their faith. They're for those who are lost. Who need to find their way back. For faltering Believers, for those who wish to believe, and for those who don't, this is for you.


God, in the easiest comparable sense is like the tradition of Santa Claus. Its a figure you've never seen but believe exists. And if your family didn't carry this tradition, or you never believed, I am sincerely sorry for how this is lost on you. For those of you who did have Santa Claus and believed, remember how much you fought tooth and nail, that kid who said he was fake. How you died on the hill defending Santa's existence? You believed with your heart and soul that Santa was real. The only proof you had was a stuffed stocking above your mantle, cookie crumbs, and a package under the tree every year. You never saw Santa, but seeing those crumbs on the plate you left out was proof enough for you. Wasn't it?


God is just like that. I mean in the sense of course that you never see Him, but believe He is there. Seeing isn't believing, ladies and gentlemen. Belief is a power we have, its beyond our control. Having proof sways your belief, but its now superficial. Its an artificial belief based on mortal evidence, not the supernatural deviation you once had. So I do this in risk of forming a superficial belief in you. But I hope I may possibly bring you back to God.


I want you to quickly look outside. See the rocks, see the trees, the grass? How did they get there? Sure the construction team that built your city probably planted them. But where did it come before the landscapers? Some forest no doubt, but where did that forest come from? From before recorded history? Well how did the Earth form? Think about the serenity of nature, the bliss, the magic of it all. Where did nature come from? The planet's inception? What caused the planet to be made? Two dust particles colliding together that caused the universe to form? Two particles of dust somehow caused atoms to emerge, violating the law of the conservation of matter, and creating life and everything as we know it? How can you explain and rationalize that? Science can't explain that, they chalk it up to dumb luck. But in science isn't luck something that can't be observed so isn't it considered, by the laws of science, to not exist? Also, if the entire universe formed from two dust particles, where did those dust particles come from? They just existed? How does that make any sense?


(I'm also making the assumption you've taken a chemistry and biology class for this next part)


The way the world works is so intricate. The way atoms combine, the properties elements have, the way our bodies function as a whole, to the organs, to tissues, to cells. It's an amazing and beautiful, and delicate balance. That's too perfect to originate from happenstance. I personally find it impossible to fathom how the world could have formed the way it did without some immortal being.


In case that didn't prove it to you, and need more proof you do, I have some more. I've seen God preform miracles for me and for others through the power of prayer.


For quick context, I was dating a girl, and after our breakup most of our friends sided with her. It felt like I was alone. But four people, Daisy, Harry, Julius, and Pinto, still talked to me after the breakup. This is all the context you need for me to help make my point here.


My relationship with this girl was on the rocks, so one night, before I went to bed, I said my prayers. As per usual. But that night I asked God to do something for me "God, in my dreams tonight show me the name of the woman that you choose for me. If she's someone I haven't met. Tell me that or give me no name at all. Show it to me carved in stone, Lord." That night, about a week before we broke up, I had a dream. In it, Daisy, Harry, Julius, Pinto and I went to go skiing. We were going to go down one of the slopes and at the base was a sign made of stone that read "The one you seek is a challenging difficult course. It's dangerous and you'll cross it alone until you and your buddies meet. Beware of trees and obstacles in your way which will slow you down."


Now that may just appear to be a normal dream. But of my friends, who I counted to be around a dozen of us who hung out together, the four with me in my dream were the only ones who spoke to me? Furthermore a stone sign? At a ski slope, in the modern age? Seems out of place for an otherwise normal dream. It seems too coincidental to be chance.


I'll give you another example. A few days after that dream, I prayed again to God asking Him to show me what to do. I listened to what He told me. I had another dream in which I was programming. I ran my code and it didn't work. But adamant there was nothing wrong with it, I ran it again. This time it worked. It fixed itself by me doing nothing. So I did precisely that. The problem solved itself. 


Now what does this all mean in the grand scheme of things? Can't these be written off as two coincidences that aligned with your prayers? They can't be. It's too improbable.


I can hear the doubters amongst you still saying "if God cares like you claim, why does He let us suffer and die?" The answer to that is simple; God doesn't do that to us. (Seen in Job 1-2). Satan does. Why doesn't God stop the beast? Because He wants to test us. To see our faith. He wants to see our flower blossom in harsh conditions.


Secondly, God makes us die because we're broken. When Adam and Eve ate of the fruit in Genesis, they became mortal. God allowed them to die. Why? If he loved us why would he let such a bad thing happen? For reasons twofold. To give our life meaning. Make every choice count. And secondly, because we're broken. God aches to see us broken and so He makes us die, so we can be fixed and perfect again in Heaven. His plan wasn't destroyed. It was changed.


See people expect God's plan to align with theirs and that's why they lose the faith, when things don't go their way. But I say to you, that God knows what's right for you, so trust Him. Follow His plan. You won't be disappointed.


It's never too late to turn to God. He loves you all the same, whether you believed your whole life, or a fraction of it. Because the Bible says that for every soul that believes will find its way into heaven; "For God so loved the world He sent His only Son, so that anyone who believes in Him might not perish, but have eternal life" (John 3:16).


Thank you for your time my friend. See you in paradise.
