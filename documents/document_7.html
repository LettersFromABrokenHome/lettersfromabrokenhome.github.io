Ladies and Gentlemen, I sit here before you today to deliver a eulogy to a game that made many a positive impact on our lives. A game that brought me a new interest into gaming, and led to a spark of my career path. A game of which many of us can only smile back upon fondly. A game which is a shell of its former self. A corporate mess that eradicated the indie love it's skeleton team gave it.


A game which I started playing when I was only 6, and grew with, as it grew with me. To see a game which I have so many fond memories of. Playing with my sisters my father and my cousins, and my friends. To have seen this game evolve and grow. To when before I was aware the concepts of updates, opening the game one day to see new content unbeknownst to me. The fantastical creatures and the random unpredictable landscape.


When I finally got the edition to play older versions a few years back it felt as looking at an old photo book from when you were young. Things you held vague memories of, photos that bring you memories and made you smile. The pains and toils of your labor. Your old habits often seen as bad now by the community, that you cannot shake because you hold hard onto the old days. The days which escaped in a blink, and escaped your clasp. Forgotten hours when you had not a care in the world. Memories that fade like old photographs. Hours you sure cannot remember what you did, but hours you remember brought you joy and made you smile.


I play the same game now, when I'm a little older. And life is a lot harder, I play it for the memories of days past. The hours I spent on the screen playing away until my mother took my tablet for the evening, are now replaced with hours staring at a monitor, delaying sleep so the morning of another rote dreadful day don't have to come. The time spent with my friends and family. The visions we had. To recreate theme parks, our town, even the world. To use it as a vessel to tell stories of our own design.


Those same stories I look back on now, those mini games we used to make. How silly those worlds seem now compared to the stuff I've made in recent years. But how proud I was at the time. To show my mother,  "Look! Look what I built!". The phrase morphed over the years to be "Look what I programmed!", of course. Therein lies one of my most sentimental connections to the game. It spawned my love of computers. The elements that let you program, the ability to mod, to add items to help tell stories formed by my ADHD powered brain.


Of course as the years changed, as did my view of the game. Growing older, I found myself more a part of the community. The trends and fads that rose and died, like the sun of a new day. The opinions as strong as a bar of steel. The hatred and fire towards the developers that burned like the fires of Hell itself. As too I also grew, the game became mocked and ridiculed. People would be harassed for playing the game and not the latest shooter. I seldom let that stop me.


Perhaps I sympathize with the developers all too much because I've been in their shoes. To work on projects that you care deeply about only for people to bully you for them. To love what you do and be proud of your work but to have it bashed.


Maybe the community never changed. Maybe it was only us that grew to see how toxic it was. But there is no denying the impact the game once had on all of us. And onto this I sign my farewell. My farewell to the community and the game itself. A shell of where it once stood. The elements People loved, the freedom to create and build. To do whatever your mind could imagine, are fading. The game feels too unlike itself. The changes never feel cool and exciting, as they once did. Why, I don't know. Maybe I never will. Maybe it's because I count down the hours until we see the new content. Maybe my imagination has died like a wounded animal. Whatever the case may be, the game is still loved by many. And as we raise our glass to the fallen soldier we once knew and love, I raise and toast to the times gone, and the times to come. That another generation will grow on this game and that it will spark the imagination and minds of millions to come.
