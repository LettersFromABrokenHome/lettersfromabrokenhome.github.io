This speech was written for one of my friends, who was too afraid to ask someone out on a date. At a later point I used the speech again for another friend. However, they had not read the same book as the first person and I had. So I had to rewrite that portion for them instead.







I'd like to tell you about the most dangerous weapon known to mankind. It's not a gun, sword, disease, or bomb. It's known as fear. It's not dangerous in the sense it will kill you like a bullet or a blade. But it's dangerous in how it's used to control you


Lets take this scenario, fictitious albeit, but a perfect example of fear as a weapon. In the famous book series The Hunger Games, by Suzzane Collins, the country of Panem controls it's subjects with fear. They take, every year, 24* children to fight to the death in a battle royale. "Winner gets fame and fortune, the loser faces certain death". This is a way fear is used to control people. It shows the citizens of Panem they can't do anything, and have to watch as their children are tortured. This fear, and display of power subdues the citizens.


Even though it has a good use you can't live in a world of bliss and ignorance. Where dogs and cats live in harmony, where there is no cruelty. The fact that fear isn't always dangerous is a somewhat comforting thought, but you can't let that be the only thing you see fear as. It can't always be an aid to you or to others. Like all things in the world, there's a bad side that humanity exploits. And humanity uses it for their own malicious benefit.


I know this seems bleak, but I promise you, this isn't a problem with no solution. In fact, all problems have solutions. Just some of them humanity hasn't found the yin to the yang... yet.


So what can you do protect yourself. There's no physical shield. You have no bullet-proof armor to this gun unfortunately. But like a plague you can build resistance to it. Like a wound you can bear a tolerance to this weapon. The first and foremost solution, and the only one I can recommend, is to stay strong. Don't let your fear show. If you show no signs of fear their weapon weakens. The shackles of fear will release.


The shackles that have no key, but you find a work around. You pick the lock, one pin at a time. Eventually it'll give way, and you'll be free. Like picking a lock, it'll take a while to undo. But you take your time, you'll get there. But be careful. One mistake and you've reset the lock. But like a monkey on a typewriter, you'll have completed the works of Shakespeare.
