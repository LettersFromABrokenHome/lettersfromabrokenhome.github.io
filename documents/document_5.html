Facts from Soup

Written on 02/26/2026

Uploaded on 02/27/2026



This speech was a reflection for myself when I found myself a victim of a nasty rumor going around. Ultimately the rumor got as far as an engineless car.  But it was still scary nonetheless to think of what could happen if it spread.







In 2006, pop-punk band Bowling for Soup released their hit song "High School Never Ends". (For censoring reasons, I'm going to be using the "Radio Disney Version" lyrics).  The chorus of the song goes;


The whole wide world is just as obsessed

With who's the best dressed and what's been suppressed

Who's got the money, who thinks they're funny

Who's kinda cute and who's just a mess

And you still don't have the right book

And you don't have the right friends

Nothing changes but the faces, the names, and the trends

High school never ends


Once I first heard that song my freshman year of high-school I thought nothing of it. Just another silly song I heard on the radio. But a few months later, come Sophomore year, I heard it again. And I thought, listening to it over and over again. The lyrics are anything but silly. They tell a message. A message to the listener. "Hold onto your butts. It gets worse from here." See, the title should tell it all. High-school never ends. People are so obsessed with who's dating who. Who's doing what. And as the chorus says. "Nothing changes but the faces the names and the trends". Instead of Gabriella Montez being everyone's obsession, it's now Taylor Swift.


See one thing that never changed from high-school. Is people never gather the facts. People read the headline and thats all they want to hear. Form their opinion on one sentence. But read inside, beyond the attention-grabbing headline, you'll find that the story inside isn't a whole lot better. More often than not, especially in those super-market magazines, are the writers taking facts and spinning them to make an interesting story. Or better yet, making assumptions, and presenting them as fact.


See, from what I've learned on my time on this mortal plane, is people only hear what they want to hear. Once someone has their opinion, their belief, no matter if the evidence points to the contrary. They won't change. Its not a can't either, it's a won't. Why people are like this, is well above my pay-grade. My best guess is it has something to do with pride. The shame to admit you were wrong. 


I digress. People only hear what they want to, and what is that? Well, ladies and gentlemen, people want to hear whatever story has the juiciest gossip. The largest glass of tea. The exaggerated truth, or the fabrication is much more entertaining than the facts. That's why most people prefer movies over documentaries. It's a fantastical world, different from our own. With caricatures of our everyday world. Those exaggerations and fabrications are what draw us in and captivate our minds. 


But this ties back to high-school. See these tabloid articles, are not far from those rumors spread back in the day. They're the hottest gossip, no matter how untrue they may be. As much as I hate to do this, Colleen Ballinger said it best, "Doesn't matter if it's true though, just as long as it's entertaining to you".


That line is the quintessence of the point I'm trying to make. People don't like facts, because they're not as entertaining as the lies they're sold. These lies can damage reputations and lives. But what do you do if someone is spreading stuff about you? What's your next move?


As bad as it sounds, you have to roll with it, go along for the ride on the "Toxic Gossip Train". Eventually it'll blow over. Eventually people won't care because a more entertaining train comes rolling along.


So remember friends, high-school never ends. But neither do your survival methods. You've made it though those four years, are currently or have them yet to come. The skills you make there will prove useful after you're done. Just remember that no matter how dark the tunnel is, theres another turn revealing the sun.
